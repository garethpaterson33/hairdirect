# HairDirect — Brand Voice v1
### Dark. Precise. Earned.

---

## The Brand in One Line

> *"Precision hair. Perfectly matched."*

---

## Who We Speak To

HairDirect is unisex by design, not by compromise. Our clients are high-earning professionals across South Africa — men experiencing hair loss, women managing alopecia or thinning, and anyone seeking precision coverage without clinical sterility or salon theatrics.

They are sceptical. They have been burned by cheap products and overconfident claims. They do their own research. They do not need convincing — they need proof.

We write for the one who is already close to a decision. The one who wants the facts, the spec, and a clear path forward. Not the one who needs hand-holding.

---

## The Voice in Practice

Think: the way a high-end optician writes. Or a precision tailor. Or an audiophile brand. They don't explain why their product is good. They describe it exactly, and let the exactness do the work.

---

## Core Principles

### 1. State. Don't explain.
We make claims without justifying them. Justification implies insecurity.

> ❌ "Our HD lace is so fine that it genuinely becomes invisible against your scalp."
> ✅ "HD lace. Invisible at the scalp line. That's the standard."

### 2. Short sentences. Then silence.
Whitespace is punctuation. The most powerful moment in our copy is what comes after a short sentence.

> ❌ "We understand that choosing a hair piece is a big decision, which is why we've made the process as simple as possible."
> ✅ "Three steps. One outcome."

### 3. Never ask for trust. Demonstrate it.
We don't say "trusted" or "reliable." We describe the thing precisely and let the precision speak.

> ❌ "Quality you can trust."
> ✅ "European Remy hair. HD Swiss lace. Matched to your scalp tone."

### 4. Name the fear. Then dismiss it.
Our clients have specific fears. We name them directly — which shows we understand them — then answer without ceremony.

> ❌ "Many people worry about whether their piece will look natural..."
> ✅ "Scepticism is rational. We've built this entire section to answer the question you haven't asked out loud yet."

### 5. No softening language.
No "perhaps," no "might," no "could." Every sentence lands flat-footed.

> ❌ "This could be the solution you've been looking for."
> ✅ "This is what you've been looking for."

### 6. Gender-neutral without being clinical.
We do not write "for men" and "for women" as separate voices. We write for the person. Hair loss, hair goals, and the desire for precision are not gendered. Our copy reflects that.

## Vocabulary

### Use
- Invisible / undetectable / engineered
- Precision / calibrated / exact / matched
- Discreet / private / quiet / unbranded
- Full life / full intensity / real conditions
- Standard / craft / specialist
- System / piece / your hair
- Partner stylist / installation / maintenance

### Never use
- Wig (in a clinical or dismissive context) / hairpiece (sounds cheap) / prosthetic / weave
- Amazing / incredible / revolutionary / game-changing
- Affordable / budget / value
- Embarrassing / problem / suffer / struggle (about the client)
- Natural-looking (implies it might not be — say "undetectable" instead)
- Don't worry (condescending)
- Exclamation marks (ever)

## Tone by Context

| Context | Register | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Hero headline | Declarative. Minimal. | *"Invisible. Undeniable. Yours."* |
| Section intro | One statement, one question answered | *"Scepticism is rational. Watch first. Decide after."* |
| Product spec | Technical, no filler | *"HD Swiss lace. Full lace cap. European Remy. Matched."* |
| Quiz intro | Direct, no pressure | *"3 minutes. Your exact match. No sales call."* |
| Testimonial framing | Cold and understated | *"Names withheld. Results are not."* |
| Partner stylist | Confident, specific | *"Verified. Trained. 30 minutes."* |
| Packaging | Minimal | *"No logo. No label. Just yours."* |
| Confirmation email | Warm but brief | *"It's on its way. Quietly."* |

## Sentence Rhythm Guide

**Hero / large display copy:** 2–5 words per phrase. Fragment sentences are fine.
> *"Invisible. Undeniable. Yours."*

**Subheads:** One complete thought. One line.
> *"Three steps. One outcome."*

**Body copy:** Max 2–3 short sentences per paragraph. Never more than 25 words in a single sentence.

**Product descriptions:** Lead with the spec. Follow with one line of context. No padding.
> *"Full lace cap. European Remy hair. Cut-in and styled by a verified partner stylist. Ready in 30 minutes."*

---

## The Brand Voice Test

Before any copy goes live, ask two questions:

1. *"Could a high-end precision brand say this?"* If yes, it's probably right.
2. *"Does this sentence need to exist?"* If the answer is no — or even maybe — cut it.

Less is not just more. Less is the brand.
